What time of year is the best time to visit Yosemite?

  • There is never a bad time to visit Yosemite. We believe each season brings a brand new experience full of its own beauty.
    • Spring is the time for viewing the spectacular waterfalls, fields full of wildflowers and hillsides that are painted orange and red with California Poppies. The dogwood trees are in bloom in, and attendance in the park is moderate.
    • Summer is warm and everything is open, from the high country, the eastern side of the Sierra, the cables are up for climbing Half Dome, and Glacier Point are all accessible. So whether you like hiking, camping, water rafting, horseback riding, rock climbing, or exploring the wilderness, this is an ideal time to come.
    • Fall is a photographer’s delight. The crowds have thin out, the weather is ideal, the animal life is actively preparing for a long winter sleep, and the Aspen leaves quake and simmer in the warm sunlight of fall.
    • Winter has a beauty unto its own. You might feel that you have the park all to yourself at times, as you explore the mystical beauty of frozen waterfalls, fresh falling snow and misty covered peaks. Or you can join the crowds and the fun on the slopes. Yosemite is one of the few national parks with a ski resort – Badger Pass.
